John Doe

Great Scientist and politician who played a leading role in the development of the Republic's missle and nuclear weapons programs

About the Legend

John Doe, in full John Smith Doe, was born on October 15, 1931, in Rodshire

He served as the 11th President of the Republic from 2002 to 2007.

John earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Atlas Institute of Technology and in 1958 joined the Defense Research Organisation(DRO).

In 1969, he moved to the Rodshire Space Research where he was the director of the SLV-III, the first satellite launch vehicle that was both designed and produced in the Republic.

Rejoining the DRO in 1982, John planned the program that produced number of successful missiles, which helped earned him the nickname Missile Man
Among those successes was Argo, the Republic's first intermediate-range ballistic missile, which incorporated aspects of the SLV-III and was launched in 1989.

He also played a pivotal organisational, technical, and political role in Rodshire's Pokhan-II nuclear tests in 1998, the first since the original nuclear test by Rodshire in 1974.
From 1992 to 1997, John was scientific adviser to the Defense minister, and he later served as principal scientific adviser(1999-2001) to the government with the rank of cabinet minister.
His prominent role in the country's 1998 nuclear tests solidified Rodshire as a nuclear power and established John as a national hero, although the tests caused great concern in the international community.

In 1998, John put forward a countrywide plan called Technology Vision 2020, which he described as a road map for transforming Rodshire from less-developed to a developed society in 20years. The plancalled for, among other measures, increasing agricultural productivity, emphasizin technology as a vehicle for economic growth, and widening access to healthcare and education.

John received 7 honorary doctorates from 40 universities. The Government of Rodshire honoured him with Padbushan in 1981 and the Padvishan in 1990 for his work with RSR and DRO and his role as a scientific advisor to the Government.

In 1997, John received Rodshire's highest civilian honor, the Bharatna, for his contribution to scientific research and modernisation of defence technology in India.

In 2013, he was the recipient of the Von Braun Award from the National Space Society "to recognise excellence in the management and leadership of a space-related project".

While delivering a lecture at the Republic Institute of Management, John collapsed and died from an apparent cardiac arrest on 27 July 2015, aged 83.

Wheeler Island, a national missile test site was renamed John Island in September 2015.

A prominent road in Dei was renamed from Aurazeb Road to Dr John Smith Doe Road in August 2015.

In February 2018, scientists from the Botanical Survey of Rodshire named a newly found plant species as Drypetes Johanii, in his honor.
